Professional Summary

AI researcher with 5+ years of industry experience in medical imaging. Developed and shipped deep learning models for retinal fundus screening products, while publishing research on out-of-distribution detection, generative data augmentation, and label-efficient learning.

Experience

  • 2021 - 2026

    Seoul, Republic of Korea

    AI Researcher (R&D Division, Fundus Team)
    VUNO, Inc.
    Developed deep learning models for the retinal fundus screening products VUNO Med-Fundus AI (on the market) and VUNO Med-Fundus Pro (upcoming), from model development and validation to release engineering. Served as Technical Research Personnel (military service alternative) from Mar 2021 to Mar 2024.
    • Upgraded the finding-classification models for the v1.2 product release — AUROC +1.48%, AUPRC +2.22%, training time reduced from 15 days to 8 hours.
    • Redesigned the input-filtering (out-of-distribution) model from one-class to binary classification — false rejections of normal images reduced from 1,359 to 21, specificity 0.9806 → 0.9997.
    • Developed disease classification (DR/AMD/RVO), lesion quantification, and integrated vessel/localization models for the upcoming product.
    • Built generative models for fundus image synthesis — text-conditioned synthesis at 1.2M-image scale (synthetic-only training on par with real-only), and mask-conditioned two-stage synthesis with LoRA fine-tuning for a 54-image lesion segmentation task (AUPRC +1.75).
    • Packaged inference runtimes as Docker images with Celery+Redis asynchronous workers for handoff to the product team; built and maintained CI/CD release pipelines with model-weight encryption; applied ONNX/OpenVINO conversion and INT8 quantization.
    • Led the team for the JustRAIGS glaucoma challenge (ISBI 2024, 1st place overall); first-authored and ran the experiments for the DRAC challenge (MICCAI 2022, 1st place in all three tasks).
    • Participated in clinical validation studies (multi-hospital reader study, confirmatory clinical trial) supporting regulatory approval.
    • Built an internal LangGraph-based multi-agent research automation harness.
